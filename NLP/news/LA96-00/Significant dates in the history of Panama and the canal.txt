

Significant dates in the history of Panama and the canal
The Associated Press
November 15, 1999, Monday, BC cycle

Section: Business News; International News
Length: 274 words
Body




1513 - Vasco Nunez de Balboa crosses the isthmus and becomes first European to see the Pacific Ocean.
1524 - Spanish conquistador Hernan Cortes suggests that a path across the isthmus would "be worth more than the conquest of Mexico."
1534 - Charles V of Spain orders survey of possible canal route across Panama.
1835 - Colombia grants France a concession to build a canal across its province of Panama.
1850 - U.S. investors begin construction of single-track railroad across Panama. It is finished five years later after an estimated 6,000 workers die.
1881 - French company begins construction of canal. Effort goes bankrupt after eight years and the deaths of some 20,000 construction workers.
1903 - Aided by U.S. military presence, Panamanians declare independence from Colombia and sign treaty granting concessions greater than Colombia had offered.
1904 - U.S. government takes over the dormant French canal project.
1914 - Canal opens.
1964 - Nationalist rioting over demand to raise Panama's flag over a school in the Canal Zone. Twenty-three Panamanians and four U.S. Marines die.
1977 - U.S. Presidents Jimmy Carter of the United States and Omar Torrijos of Panama sign treaty handing over canal to Panama in 1999. U.S. Senate ratifies treaty the following year.
1979 - United States transfers Panama Canal Zone, the 50-mile strip of land along the banks of the canal, to Panamanian control.
1989 - U.S. invades Panama to overthrow dictator Manuel Noriega.
1990 - Fernando Manfredo becomes first Panamanian administrator of the canal - a post previously held exclusively by U.S. citizens.
Dec. 31, 1999 - Panama to take over possession of canal.
