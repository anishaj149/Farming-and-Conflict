

Developments in the aftermath of the Sept. 11 attacks
December 19, 2001, Wednesday, BC cycle

Length: 178 words
Byline: By The Associated Press
Body




Developments Wednesday related to the Sept. 11 attacks:
- Zacarias Moussaoui, the first person indicted in the attacks, briefly appears in the a suburban Washington courthouse. He is accused of conspiring to murder thousands.
- New York Gov. George Pataki says the underground fires that have burned at the World Trade Center site for the past three months have finally been extinguished.
- Troop reinforcements move into the remote hills of central Yemen, where government forces have battled armed tribesmen in an assault to capture suspected operatives of Osama bin Laden.
- U.S. fighter jets at the forefront of the bombing campaign in Afghanistan have reduced combat sorties and focused on reconnaissance following the fall of Tora Bora, the U.S. military says.
- Belgium has arrested a man of Tunisian descent on suspicion of recruiting fighters for Osama bin Laden's al-Qaida network, the public prosecutor's office says.
- University of South Florida's trustees say a Palestinian professor linked to known terrorists should be fired for disrupting university operations.
