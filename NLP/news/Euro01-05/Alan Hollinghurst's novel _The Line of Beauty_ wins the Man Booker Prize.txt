

Alan Hollinghurst's novel "The Line of Beauty" wins the Man Booker Prize

October 19, 2004, Tuesday, BC cycle

Section: International News
Length: 86 words
Dateline: LONDON
Body




Alan Hollinghurst's novel "The Line of Beauty" won the Man Booker Prize, Britain's most prestigious literary award, on Tuesday.
The British writer was chosen over five other authors - from Britain, Ireland and South Africa - for the $89,500 award.
The Man Booker Prize is open to writers from Britain, Ireland and the Commonwealth of former British colonies.
The judging panel included novelist Tibor Fischer, writer and academic Robert Macfarlane, Erotic Review editor Rowan Pelling and Labour Party lawmaker Chris Smith.
