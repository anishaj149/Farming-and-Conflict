The men who verified the IRA disarmament:
 
JOHN DE CHASTELAIN: A retired Canadian general. Born in Romania in 1937, he was educated in England and Scotland and later moved to Canada, where he joined the Royal Canadian Infantry Corps and attended the Royal Military College in Ontario.
He helped mediate a crisis between the Mohawk Indians and Canadian officials in 1990; some Mohawk weapons were decommissioned. Assisted U.S. Sen. George Mitchell, in negotiations that led to the Northern Ireland peace accord of 1998. Named chairman of the Independent International Commission on Decommissioning.
 
THE REV. HAROLD GOOD, 68: A Methodist minister from Londonderry, Northern Ireland, he has served congregations in the Republic of Ireland, the United States and Northern Ireland. He has also worked as a chaplain in the Crumlin Road Prison in Belfast. In 2001 he was elected president of the Methodist Church in Ireland. He was a member of the Northern Ireland Human Rights Commission from 1999 to 2004.
 
THE REV. ALEC REID, 74: Roman Catholic priest originally from Dublin. Friend and confessor of Sinn Fein leader Gerry Adams, he has participated in several "peace-making" ministries since 1969, trying to bridge the gap between Catholics and Protestants. He also worked in ministries to help prisoners and families.
