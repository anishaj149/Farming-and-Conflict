

A look at the members of the U.N. Security Council

Body




Here is a look at the 15 members of the U.N. Security Council.
There are five permanent members with veto power:
-The United States
-Russia
-China
-Britain
-France
 
There are 10 non-permanent members selected by regional groups for two-year terms. They do not have veto power:
-Bulgaria
-Cameroon
-Colombia
-Guinea
-Ireland
-Mauritius
-Mexico
-Norway
-Singapore
-Syria
 
The presidency of the Security Council rotates every month in alphabetical order. Bulgaria is the current president.
A resolution requires a minimum of nine "yes" votes, and no veto by a permanent member.
