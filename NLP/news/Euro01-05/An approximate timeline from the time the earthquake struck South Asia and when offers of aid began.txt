

An approximate timeline from the time the earthquake struck South Asia and when offers of aid began to arrive
The Associated Press
October 10, 2005, Monday, BC cycle

Section: International News
Length: 408 words
Byline: By The Associated Press
Body




- Saturday, 8:50 a.m.: Earthquake hits the divided Kashmir region on the border of Pakistan and India.
- 9 a.m.: Pakistan Red Crescent ambulances join local relief efforts in Islamabad.
- 12 p.m.: Two local Pakistan Red Crescent groups deploy to Pakistan's Northwest Frontier province and another one to Kashmir to assess the damage but are delayed because roads have been destroyed.
- 2 p.m. The World Food Program holds an emergency meeting in Pakistan but cannot dispatch teams because of the roads. WFP begins working to send helicopters.
- 5 p.m., or 2 p.m. in Brussels: The European Union offers to send emergency humanitarian aid to the quake-stricken regions. The Turkish Red Crescent prepares to send military planes loaded with supplies.
- 8 p.m., or 3 p.m. Greenwich Mean Time: Germany's Foreign Minister Joschka Fischer says Berlin has sent $61,000 for emergency aid. Austria says it too is ready to send supplies.
- 7 p.m., or 10 a.m in New York: U.N. Secretary-General Kofi Annan announces that the United Nations has offered help to the governments of Afghanistan, India and Pakistan.
- 10 p.m., or about 1 p.m. in Washington : President Bush offers condolences and Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice says the United States is ready to help. The United States wires an initial $100,000 in aid and dispatches 250 rolls of plastic sheeting, 5,000 blankets, and 5,000 water containers, to arrive Monday.
- Just before midnight, or 7 p.m. in Geneva: First eight members of U.N. Disaster Assessment and Coordination team leaves Switzerland for Islamabad.
- Sunday, 8 a.m.: Maj. Gen. Shaukat Sultan says confirmed death toll is 18,000.
- Morning-afternoon: Rescue crews arrive from Russia, Britain, the United Arab Emirates, and China with sniffer dogs, earth moving equipment, medicines, food and relief goods.
- 7 p.m., or 10 a.m. in Washington: The United States sends another $1 million.
- 2 p.m.: U.S. ambassador to Pakistan says the White House has authorized $50 million in earthquake aid for Pakistan.
- 3 p.m.: First U.S. helicopters that will help in relief efforts land near Pakistani capital.
- 9 p.m.: Pakistan agrees to accept relief aid for quake victims from rival India.
- 9:30 p.m., or 12:30 p.m. in New York: The U.N. Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs announces that 12 nations have sent rescue teams: China, France, Germany, Greece, Japan, the Netherlands, Poland, Russia, Singapore, South Korea, Turkey, and Ukraine.
