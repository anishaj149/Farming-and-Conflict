

Stamps in the News: Postal Service hails evolution of flight
June 27, 1997, Friday, BC cycle

Length: 524 words
Byline: By SYD KRONISH
Body




The Wright Brothers' flying machine started it in 1903. But, since that time, American aviation has soared to great heights and accomplishments in a never-ending saga of the skies.
The U.S. Postal Service pays tribute to the legendary aviation pioneers and warime heroes with 20 new commemorative stamps depicting the evolution of flight. The "Classic American Aircraft" stamps will feature the evolution of flight via illustrations of America's civilian and military aircraft through the years.
The aircraft appearing on the stamps are: The North American P-51 Mustang, the "Model B" Flyer used by the Army in 1911, the Piper J-3 Cub, the Lockheed Vega, the Northrup Alpha, the Martin B-10 bomber, the Navy's Corsair F4U, the Boeing B-47 Stratojet, the Gee Bee Super Spotter, the Beech Model C17L Staggerwing, the Boeing B-17 Flying Fortress, the Stearman PT-13 Trainer, the Lockheed Constellation, the Lockheed P-38 Lightning fighter, the P-26 Peashooter fighter, the Ford Tri-Motor, The Doublas DC-3 transport, the Boeing 314 Clipper, the Curtiss JN-4 Jenny trainer, the Grumman F4F Wildcat fighter.
The new stamps will have first-day issuance on July 21 in Dayton, Ohio. On July 21 the stamps will be available at post offices nationwide and will be sold only in panes of 20.
First-day cancellations can be obtaind by mail by purchasing the panes at your local post ffice, affix the stamps to your envelopes, address the envelopes and place in a larger envelope, addressed to: Classic American Aircraft Stamps, Postmaster, 1111 East 5th St., Dayton, Ohio, 45401-9991.
Your orders must be postmarked no later than Aug. 19.
The sheet of 32-cent stamps was designed by Phil Jordan of Falls Church, Va. and illustrated by internationally renownd aviation artist William Phillips. The stamps are the fifth "Classic Collection" issued by the Postal Service.

First-Day covers offered
The Philatelic Fullment Service Center also offers first-day covers for new issues such as the Classic American Aircraft stamps. The covers are postmarked with the official first day of issue cancellation. Each item has a catalog number and is offered in the quarterly "USA Philatelic" catalog. You can request a free catalog by writing to: Information Fulfillment, U.S. Postal Service, Dept. 6270, P.O. Box 419014, Kansas City, Mo. 64141-6104. Or you may call 800-STAMP-24.

Mali stamps honor leading ladies, directors
Movie buffs will be interested in a recent isue by Mali, a republic in West Africa. The stamps honor six of Hollywod's famous leading ladies and their film directors.
The 200 francs stamps feature the following: Marlene Dietrich and director Josef von Sternberg of the 1930 film "The Blue Angel"; Jean Harlow and director George Cukor of the 1933 classic "Dinner at Eight"; Mary Astor and director John Huston of the 1942 picture "The Maltese Falcon"; Ingrid Berman and director Alfred Hitchcock of the 1945 movie "Spellbound"; Claudette Colbert and director Cecil B. DeMille of the 1934 production of "Cleopatra"; and Marilyn Monroe and director Billy Wilder of the 1959 hit "Some Like It Hot."
The stamps of Mali are available at your local dealer.
