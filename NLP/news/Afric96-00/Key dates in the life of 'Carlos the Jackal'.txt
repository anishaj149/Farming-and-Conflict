Key dates in the life of 'Carlos the Jackal'
---
1949: Ilich Ramirez Sanchez, the real name of Carlos, is born in Venezuela to a wealthy communist lawyer who gave his son Lenin's middle name.
1964: Joins Communist Students Movement in Venezuela.
Goes for guerrilla training in Cuba.
1968: Begins study at Patrice Lumumba University in Moscow, famous as training ground for future terrorists and KGB recruits.
1970: Joins the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine. Begins terrorist career. He later is dubbed "Carlos" after the fictional terrorist in Frederick Forsyth's 1971 novel "The Day of the Jackal."
1970-1982: Key attacks linked to Ramirez include massacre of Israeli athletes at Munich Olympics, seizing OPEC ministers in Vienna, hijacking of Air France plane to Entebbe, half-a-dozen attacks on French targets.
1992: Convicted by a French court in absentia for the 1975 killings of two French counterintelligence agents.
1994: Ramirez arrested in Sudan and transferred to France, where he is jailed in solitary confinement in a maximum-security prison.
1997: Stands trial for the first time in person, charged again with the 1975 killings of two French counterintelligence agents.
