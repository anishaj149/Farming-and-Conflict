Pope Benedict XVI tapped two Americans on Wednesday in naming 23 new cardinals. The nominations bring the total number of American cardinals to 17, 13 of whom are under age 80 and thus eligible to vote in a conclave. Here is a list:
1. Archbishop Daniel DiNardo of Galveston-Houston, Texas, named Wednesday.
2. Archbishop John Foley, grand master of the Equestrian Order of the Holy Sepulcher of Jerusalem, named Wednesday.
3. Cardinal Edward Egan, archbishop of New York.
4. Cardinal Francis George, archbishop of Chicago.
5. Cardinal William Keeler, retired archbishop of Baltimore.
6. Cardinal Bernard Law, archpriest of Rome's Basilica of St. Mary Major and retired archbishop of Boston.
7. Cardinal William Levada, prefect of the Congregation for the Doctrine of the Faith.
8. Cardinal Roger Mahony, archbishop of Los Angeles.
9. Cardinal Adam Maida, archbishop of Detroit.
10. Cardinal Theodore McCarrick, retired archbishop of Washington D.C.
11. Cardinal Sean O'Malley, archbishop of Boston.
12. Cardinal Justin Rigali, archbishop of Philadelphia.
13. Cardinal J. Francis Stafford, head of the Apostolic Penitentiary.
There are also four American cardinals who are over age 80. They are:
1. Cardinal William Baum, former archbishop of Washington and former head of the Apostolic Penitentiary.
2. Cardinal Anthony Bevilacqua, retired archbishop of Philadelphia.
3. Cardinal Avery Dulles, Jesuit theologian.
4. Cardinal Edmund Szoka, former governor of Vatican City.
