

Developments in the aftermath of the Sept. 11 attacks
January 21, 2002, Monday, BC cycle

Length: 160 words
Byline: By The Associated Press
Body




Developments Monday related to the Sept. 11 attacks:
- Fourteen detainees from Afghanistan arrive on stretchers at the Guantanamo Naval Base in Cuba as new questions arise about the treatment and legal status of inmates.
- Afghan Prime Minister Hamid Karzai collects promises of $2.6 billion over three years to rebuild his shattered country at a Tokyo conference; the United Nations estimates $15 billion will be needed in the next decade.
- U.S. officials say John Walker Lindh, the American found fighting alongside the Taliban, will likely leave Tuesday for the United States, where he faces trial on charges of conspiring to kill fellow countrymen.
- A man is arrested in the southern Philippines for allegedly belonging to an al-Qaida-linked group accused of plotting terror attacks in Southeast Asia.
- U.S. District Court Judge A. Howard Matz agrees to consider a petition by civil rights advocates challenging the detention of terrorism suspects at Guantanamo.
