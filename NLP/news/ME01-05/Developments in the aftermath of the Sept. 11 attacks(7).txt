

Developments in the aftermath of the Sept. 11 attacks
January 4, 2002, Friday, BC cycle

Body




Developments Friday related to the Sept. 11 attacks:
-A Green Beret is killed by small-arms fire in eastern Afghanistan, the first death of a member of the U.S. military by enemy fire in the campaign; a CIA officer is wounded in the shootout but expected to survive.
-The Afghan foreign minister says Mullah Mohammad Omar, once the Taliban's supreme leader, is surrounded by anti-Taliban forces near Baghran.
-U.S. warplanes bomb for a second straight day an al-Qaida cave complex in eastern Afghanistan; Gen. Tommy Franks, commander of U.S. Central Command, says U.S. forces have searched seven of the eight most interesting cave complexes around Tora Bora and 40 out of 48 identified terrorist training camps or bases in Afghanistan.
-A defense official says the U.S. has arranged for Pakistan to turn over the Taliban's former ambassador, Mullah Abdul Salam Zaeef.
-Zacarias Moussaoui, the man charged as an accomplice in the Sept. 11 attacks, asks a judge to allow his trial to be televised to ensure the proceedings are fair.
