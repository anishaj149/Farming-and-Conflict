

Developments in the aftermath of the Sept. 11 attacks
January 16, 2002, Wednesday, BC cycle

Length: 228 words
Byline: By The Associated Press
Body




Developments Wednesday related to the Sept. 11 attacks:
- The Pentagon sends more than 200 troops to the Philippines to help local forces fighting an extremist group linked to al-Qaida.
- Richard Reid, the airline passenger accused of trying to ignite explosives in his shoes, is indicted on charges of being an al-Qaida-trained terrorist.
- President Bush encourages Turkey's offer to lead peacekeeping in Afghanistan but puts off a decision on whether to help finance the operation.
- Senate Majority Leader Tom Daschle and a congressional delegation meet Afghanistan's prime minister in Kabul, promising U.S. involvement won't end with the conflict.
- U.S. officials tentatively conclude Osama bin Laden's terrorist group had not developed the means to produce chemical, biological or radiological weapons when the United States began bombing Afghanistan.
- Investigators question a man who described himself as a financial supporter of the Taliban and showed up at a U.S. base in Afghanistan offering information.
- After collecting $425 million in four months, officials at The September 11th Fund call for a halt on donations saying they'll concentrate on distributing the money.
- President Bush signs legislation naming a Cranbury, N.J., post office after Todd Beamer, a passenger on the hijacked plane that crashed in Pennsylvania; Beamer uttered the words "Let's roll."
