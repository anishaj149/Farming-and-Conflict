

The Main Issues In The Peace Talks

October 24, 1991, Thursday, BC cycle

Section: International News
Length: 134 words
Dateline: NICOSIA, Cyprus
Body




Here are the main issues to be discussed at the Mideast peace conference in Madrid.
 
 ISRAEL
Israel says it will not trade land for peace.
It wants separate talks with Arab states and formal recognition from them.
 
 PALESTINIANS
The Palestinians demand an independent homeland in the occupied West Bank and Gaza with a capital in Jerusalem.
 
 SYRIA
Syria wants Israel to return the Golan Heights.
 
 EGYPT
Cairo wants Israel to give up all occupied territory and insists this must be the basis for negotiations.
 
 JORDAN
Jordan supports demands for a Palestinian homeland in the occupied lands.
It also wants an agreement with Israel on water rights, long an issue between the countries.
 
 LEBANON
Lebanon wants Israel to withdraw from a border zone occupied since 1982.
It also wants to talk about scarce water resources.
