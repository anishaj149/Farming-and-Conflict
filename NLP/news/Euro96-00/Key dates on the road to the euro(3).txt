

Key dates on the road to the euro

March 1979 - European Monetary System is launched to protect EU currencies from wild fluctuations.
Dec. 9-10, 1991 - New EU treaty lays groundwork for single currency.
Aug. 1, 1993 - Speculation against French franc breaks EMS apart, its restrictions on currency fluctuations are relaxed.
Dec. 15-16, 1995 - EU leaders agree to launch the euro in 1999 for countries with low deficits, debt, inflation and interest rates.
Dec. 13-14, 1996 - EU leaders unveil euro note designs, featuring bridges, windows and doorways in various European styles.
June 16-17, 1997 - EU leaders unveil coin designs for euros and euro-cents.
March 25, 1998 - European Commission to recommend 11 nations adopt euro - Germany, France, Italy, Belgium, Netherlands, Luxembourg, Ireland, Portugal, Spain, Austria and Finland. Britain, Denmark, Sweden opt out. Greece fails to meet the euro's economic criteria.
May 2, 1998 - EU leaders expected to endorse choice of euro nations, set exchange rates and name director of European Central Bank.
Jan. 1, 1999 - Euro nations lock exchange rates of national money. Governments, businesses start keeping accounts in euros; EU central bank starts running EU monetary policy.
Jan. 1, 2002 - Euro comes into common circulation alongside national currencies during a six-month period after which national currencies disappear.
