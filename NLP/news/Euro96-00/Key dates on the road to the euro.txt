

Key dates on the road to the euro

March 1979 - European Monetary System launched to protect EU currencies from wild fluctuations.
Dec. 9-10, 1991 - New EU treaty lays groundwork for single currency.
Aug. 1, 1993 - Speculation against French franc breaks apart European Monetary System; restrictions on currency fluctuations are relaxed.
Dec. 15-16, 1995 - EU leaders agree to launch the "euro" in 1999 for countries with low deficits, debt, inflation and interest rates.
Dec. 13-14, 1996 - EU leaders unveil euro note designs, featuring bridges, windows and doorways in various European styles.
June 16-17, 1997 - EU leaders unveil euro coin designs.
March 25, 1998 - European Commission recommends 11 countries adopt euro - Germany, France, Italy, Belgium, the Netherlands, Luxembourg, Ireland, Portugal, Spain, Austria and Finland. Britain, Denmark, Sweden opt out. Greece fails to meet the economic criteria.
May 2, 1998 - EU leaders expected to endorse choice of euro countries, set exchange rates and name director of European Central Bank.
Jan. 1, 1999 - Euro countries lock exchange rates between their various currencies and between each of those currencies and the euro. Governments, businesses start keeping accounts in euros; EU central bank starts running EU monetary policy.
Jan. 1, 2002 - Euro comes into common circulation alongside national currencies during a six-month period after which national currencies disappear.
