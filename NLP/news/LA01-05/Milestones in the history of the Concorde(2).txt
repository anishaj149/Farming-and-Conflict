

Milestones in the history of the Concorde

Key events in the history of the Concorde:
-Nov. 29, 1962: French and British governments sign agreement to develop prototypes for supersonic transport aircraft. Aerospatiale of France and British Aircraft Corporation Ltd. (later British Aerospace PLC) sign similar pact same day.
-December 1967: First test version, Concorde 001, unveiled by Aerospatiale.
-March 2, 1969: First test flight from Toulouse, southern French city where Concorde built, to Le Bourget Airport near Paris.
-Dec. 3, 1973: Maiden flight of first commercial-production Concorde.
-Jan. 21, 1976: First Concorde passenger flights: British Airways Concorde from London to Bahrain and Air France from Paris to Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.
-May 24, 1976: British Airways and Air France begin Concorde service to Washington from London and Paris.
-Oct. 17, 1977: U.S. Supreme Court overrules New York Port Authority ban on Concorde flights in New York area; regular service to and from Kennedy Airport begins later in year.
-Oct. 8, 1998: Large piece of Concorde rudder falls off during flight from London to New York; plane lands safely.
-July 23, 2000: British Airways discloses cracks found in wings of all seven of its Concorde jets. Next day Air France announces cracks in four of its six Concordes.
-July 25, 2000: Air France Concorde en route to New York crashes outside Paris shortly after takeoff, killing all 109 people on board and four people on ground. All Air France Concordes grounded.
-Aug. 10, 2000: French investigators say metal strip on runway apparently damaged tire and debris punctured fuel tank, starting fire.
-Nov. 7, 2001: British Airways and Air France resume Concorde service to New York with reinforced fuel tanks and tires.
-April 10, 2003: Air France, British Airways announce Concordes being retired.
-May 31, 2003: Air France's final Concorde commercial flight, New York to Paris.
-Oct. 24, 2003: British Airway's final commerical flight, New York to London.
