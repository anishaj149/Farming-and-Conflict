

A country-by-country breakdown of the death toll from the earthquake, tsunami in the Indian Ocean
The Associated Press
December 31, 2004, Friday, BC cycle

Section: International News
Length: 193 words
Byline: By The Associated Press
Body




At least 121,562 people were killed in 11 countries in southern Asia and Africa from Sunday's massive earthquake and tsunamis, according to official figures. A breakdown of the toll so far:
- Indonesia: At least 80,246 people were killed on Sumatra island, the government said.
The health minister said Friday the country's toll could rise to 100,000.
- Sri Lanka: Some 28,551 killed. About 1 million people were displaced.
- India: The government said 7,763 deaths have been confirmed but nearly 4,000 more were missing in India's remote Andaman and Nicobar Islands, just north of Sumatra.
- Thailand: The government said 4,560 people died, including 2,230 foreigners.
- Somalia: At least 200 killed, said Somali presidential spokesman Yusuf Mohamed Ismail.
- Myanmar: About 90 people were killed, according to reports compiled by international aid agencies.
- Maldives: At least 73 people confirmed dead.
- Malaysia: At least 66 people, including an unknown number of foreign tourists, were dead, according to official reports.
- Tanzania: At least 10 people killed, said Alfred Tibaigana, police commander in Dar es Salaam.
- Bangladesh: Two killed.
- Kenya: One killed.
