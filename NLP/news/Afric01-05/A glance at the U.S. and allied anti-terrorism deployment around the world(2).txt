MAIN FRONTS
IRAQ: Nearly 140,000 U.S. soldiers, 11,000 British and 9,500 others under Polish control from 21 countries.
AFGHANISTAN: NATO leads 5,000 peacekeepers from 30 nations in alliance's first operation outside Europe.
Mission limited mostly to capital, Kabul, but U.S. and Afghan forces battle remnants of toppled Taliban around country.
PERSIAN GULF: About 2,000 U.S. troops stationed in Qatar, emirate that hosted command center for Iraq war. U.S. Navy 5th Fleet based in Bahrain.
PHILIPPINES: United States giving counterterrorism training and aid to military in fight against Muslim extremist group Abu Sayyaf, which officials have loosely linked to al-Qaida.
---
REAR GUARD
CENTRAL ASIA: U.S. troops at air base in Uzbekistan. U.S.-led anti-terror coalition at civilian airport in Kyrgyzstan.
DJIBOUTI: More than 1,800 U.S. troops with anti-terrorism task force for the Horn of Africa.
EAST AFRICA: U.S. 5th Fleet expanding patrols in region. Eleven nations join U.S.-led terrorism response force.
CAUCASUS: Pentagon provides military aid to Azerbaijan. U.S. troops training Georgia's military in anti-terrorist operations.
