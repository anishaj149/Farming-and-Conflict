

Big Movers in the Stock Market
The Associated Press
December 22, 1994, Thursday, AM cycle

Section: Business News
Length: 585 words
Dateline: NEW YORK
Body




Some of the stocks that moved substantially or traded heavily Thursday on the New York Stock Exchange, Nasdaq Stock Market, and American Stock Exchange.
 
 NYSE
Telefonos de Mexico's American depositary receipts fell 4 to 40 .
YPF Sociedad fell to 21 1/8.
Grupo Tribasa fell 2 1/2 to 19 .
Grupo Televisa fell 3 to 32 .
Empresas ICA fell 3 to 20 .
Mexican and other Latin American stocks plummetted after the Mexican government allowed a devaluation of the peso.
 
 Citicorp fell 2 to 41 1/8.
Bank of Boston fell 1/2 to 26 1/2.
S.G. Warburg downgraded Citicorp to "hold" from "buy," saying losses in Latin American could result in a selloff in Citicorp shares of as much as 10 percent over the near term. Analysts said Bank of Boston had a large exposure to Latin America debt.
 
 Coca-Cola fell 1 to 51 .
Philip Morris fell 1/2 to 56 .
Colgate-Palmolive fell 1 to 61 .
The stocks of exporters fell on Mexico's troubles.
 
 Carnival Corp. fell 1 to 19 .
The company reported fourth-quarter profits of 25 cents per share, up from 18 cents a year earlier and in line with expectations. But Smith Barney lowered its estimate of the company's first-quarter profits by 3 cents per share to 23 cents, citing slowing near-term momentum.
 
 Bankers Trust rose to 56 .
The company's securities unit agreed to pay a $ 10 million civil penalty to settle federal regulator investigations of its derivatives dealings, ending the probes of the big New York bank brought by the Securities and Exchange Commission and the Commodities Futures Trading Commission.
 
 Owens-Illinois fell 1 to 11.
The New Jersey Supreme Court reversed a lower-court ruling that would have allowed the Toledo, Oh. maker of plastics and glass packaging products to seek up to $ 600 million in insurance reimbursement for asbestos-related claims.
 

 
 NASDAQ
Centocor rose 1 1/2 to 18 1/8.
The company's Centocor B.V. unit was granted a license from the Food and Drug Administration for its ReoPro drug. The heart drug will be marketed and distributed in the U.S. by Eli Lilly.
 
 Avid Technology fell 5 to 36.
Oppenheimer downgraded the stock, saying increased competition in the audio-visual editing business will cause Avid to fall short of Wall Street's earnings expectations in 1995.
 
 Salick rose 5 to 34 .
London-based Zeneca Group said it would buy 50 percent of Salick for $ 195 million in cash.
 
 Broderbund Software rose 4 to 44 .
The home-entertainment software company's first-quarter net income of 57 cents per share was much higher than analysts' expectations of 39 cents and the year-ago profits of 31 cents. The company cited good sales of CD-ROMs.
 
 Tele-Communications "A" rose 9-16 to 21 13-16.
Microsoft fell to 60 .
Tele-Communications said it would invest $ 125 million for a 20-percent stake in Microsoft's online services business.
 
 Borland International fell to 6 .
The software company said it expected a "substantial" operating loss and lower revenues in the third quarter and announced the resignation of turnaround specialist and chief operating officer Keith Maib.
 

 
 AMEX
InterDigital rose 1/2 to 5 1/2.
The stock was rising for the second consecutive session after Siemens said it would pay InterDigital $ 20 million to enter into an alliance based on InterDigital's UltraPhone digital wireless products.
 
 Greyhound Line rose 3-16 to 2.
More than 90 percent of Greyhound's bondholders agreed to swap their debt for stock in the company. Greyhound had said that if 90 percent of the investors didn't go along it would take the reorganization effort to bankruptcy court.
