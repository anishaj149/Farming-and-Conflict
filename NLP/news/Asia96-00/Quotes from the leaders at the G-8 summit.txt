

Quotes from the leaders at the G-8 summit
The Associated Press
June 21, 1999, Monday, PM cycle

Section: International News
Length: 237 words
Byline: By the Associated Press
Body




"The most important thing is to mend ties after a fight." - Russian President Boris Yeltsin, referring to his repeated confrontations with NATO over the air campaign in Yugoslavia.
---
"We can't create or guarantee peace in Europe without Russia." - German Chancellor Gerhard Schroeder.
 
 ---
"All of this of course will cost money. But how well spent that money will be. The costliest peace is cheaper than the cheapest war." -President Clinton, calling for a plan to stabilize southeastern Europe now that Kosovo conflict is ended.
 
 ---
"It shows democracies are capable of saying no to barbarism and racism." - French President Jacques Chirac, on resolution of Kosovo crisis.
 
 ---
"He has ruined their country, their economy is crippled, they are an outcast in the world. Why keep him?" - British Prime Minister Tony Blair, making the case for Milosevic's removal on ABC's "This Week."
 
 ---
"Strong, but he can't run a marathon." - Canadian Prime Minister Jean Chretien when asked to assess Yeltsin's health.
 
 ---
"He read his speech with a loud voice. But then I've spoken to him many times on the phone, and he always speaks with a loud voice." - Italian Prime Minister Massimo D'Alema, on same question.
 
 ---
"I am tackling the task with a resolute determination to ensure that recent positive signs of economic growth become more solid and take root." - Japanese Prime Minister Keizo Obuchi on efforts to boost Japan's economy.
